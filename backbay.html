Back Bay Fens & Charlesgate Park

Image credit: Catherine Sherman, 2018

Back Bay Fens was designed by Olmsted in the late 1800s. It is a main feature of Boston’s Emerald Necklace system that covers over 1000 acres. Prior to the development of Highway 90, Charlesgate, also designed by Olmsted in the late 1800s, connected the Emerald Necklace and the Charles River. It is now broken into pieces by Highway 90 and other roadways. Along the south side of Back Bay Fens and along the Charles River further to the north, the 2012 Median Household income was $10,446-$30,000 and the area just north of the park has a median income slightly higher from $30,000 - $53,1336 (the overall median income of the City of Boston) (source: Boston Planning & development agency). The Fenway and Kenmore neighborhoods surrounding the Back Bay Fens are quite dense, with many census blocks having over 500 people per acre (source: Boston Planning & development agency).

Walking Tour of Back Bay Fens & Commonwealth Avenue Mall

Starting at the wide open and hot Fenway MBTA stop, this walking tour will take you through the Back Bay Fens park and down Commonwealth Avenue towards Boston Public Garden. The high temperature and dry Fenway MBTA stop will be soon long forgotten as you enter the urban oasis that is Back Bay Fens. This park, surrounded by trees, water features, and gardens, remains between 75-85F as you walk through. The coolest parts of the park are when you are near the Muddy River. As soon as you cross over into Charlesgate (surrounded by highway overpasses and other roadways), the temperatures soar. Once you hit Commonwealth Avenue, these temperatures flatten as you experience temperatures around 85F. This will drop as you enter the Public Garden, explored here. Along this pathway, you will notice there is storm flooding, especially alongside the water featuers that exist.

Excess Heat Analysis

The inner regions of both Back Bay Fens and Charlesgate Park are relatively cool compared to the areas along the outer edges of the park. As can be seen, the neighborhood directly east of the Fens is almost 9° Celsius warmer than the core of the park. To the west and south, this extreme heat is slightly mollified and is between 5-7° Celsius warmer than the coolest temperature in the park, which is 25° Celsius.
