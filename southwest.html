Southwest Corridor Park

Image credit: curbed Boston, 2018

The Southwest Corridor Park, which aligns with the Orange Line MBTA track from Back Bay through Roxbury and Jamaica Plain to Forest Hills, is 4.1 miles long, extremely skinny, and covers 52 acres. According to the 2012 ACS 5-Year Estimates, the median income along this park ranges from $10,445 - $30,000 in the northern parts to $53,136-$100,000 as it moves south [1]. Population density along the Southwest Corridor Park decreases steadily as you go south along it, with the South End neighborhood start of the northern end of the park having densities above 51-100 people per acre and the lower neighborhoods having anywhere from 0-50 people per acre (source: Boston Planning & development agency).

Southwest Corridor Walking Tour

As a park going through the core of inland Boston, the Southwest Corridor Linear Park does not experience any flooding. Nevertheless, its unique shape, in particular its very narrow width, means that it does not enjoy as much of the benefit of cooling due to treecover and greenspace as other parks explored in this project. Starting at Back Bay Station in the North, individuals who traverse the four miles of the park will experience temperatures over 85F for the first 1.5 miles of the trail. Yet, no matter where you are on the trail, temperatures never drop below 80F. The lowest temperatures occur in four places. Approximately 1.5 miles into the trail, an elevation peak is reached and temperatures drop by around 4F. At 2.4 miles, temperatures drop about 3F as you enter a small culvert with an elevation drop of 14 feet. At the highest point of the trail, temperatures drop to almost 82F. The lowest temperatures on the trail, at around 3.7 miles, is at a wider portion of the park.
