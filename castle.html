Castle Island

Image credit: Boston Cityside Realty

Castle Island is home to the 19th Century Fort Independence and covers 22 acres on the edge of Boston Harbor. It has been a site of fortification since the 1634. Although immediately surrounded by unpopulated census tracts, the neighborhood directly west of Castle Island (South Boston) has a median income of $71,738-$100,000 (source: Boston Planning & development agency). The neighborhood to the west of Castle Island is denser than those at the southern end of the Southwest Corridor Park, with an average density at around 51-100 persons per acre (source: Boston Planning & development agency).

Walking Tour of Castle Island & the Strandaway

Starting at the Andrew MBTA station, anyone who seeks to walk the almost two miles to Castle Island will bake in the high temperatures. This will be come a figment of the past once you enter Castle Island, which enjoys sea breezes due to its prominent location on Boston Harbor. Even with little tree cover, the park remains cool. Beyond coolness, the park will experience significant flooding in the case of a storm event and sea level rise. Flooding is minimized where the Fort itself stands, but the rest of the Strandaway and Castle Island itself will be under 6 feet of flooding.

Inundation Analysis

As expected from the walking tour of Castle Island, when faced with inundation the island becomes once again an island. The core of Castle Island, Fort Independence, remains above water due to its position on a promontory. Areas closes to Castle Island also experience moderate flooding, though much of Dorchester Heights and City Point remain above water.
